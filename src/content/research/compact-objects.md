We develop and apply general-relativistic particle-in-cell (GRPIC) methods to study the extreme plasma environments surrounding black holes and neutron stars. Our research focuses on magnetic reconnection in black hole magnetospheres, the formation and acceleration of relativistic jets, and the mechanisms responsible for high-energy emission from compact objects. By combining curved-spacetime electrodynamics with kinetic plasma physics, we explore how particles are accelerated to ultra-relativistic energies in the vicinity of event horizons and in the ergosphere of rotating black holes.
