I am fascinated by high-energy astrophysical plasmas such as those found around black holes and neutron stars. To investigate plasma phenomena in these environments, leading to potentially observable emission, I use first-principles as well as fluid approaches to plasma simulations.

My latest work on plasma turbulence in astrophysical accretion disks, using fully kinetic simulations to capture all the cross-scale physics involved in the process, has been recently published in Physical Review Letters for <a href="https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.133.045202" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">local ("shearing-box")</a> and <a href="https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.130.115201" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">global simulations</a>. I have also worked on general-relativistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations of accreting and <a href="https://doi.org/10.3847/1538-4357/ababab" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">flaring plasmas around black holes</a>, as well as more localized, fundamental phenomena such as <a href="https://doi.org/10.3847/1538-4357/acd0b" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">relativistic magnetic reconnection</a> and <a href="https://doi.org/10.3847/1538-4365/aafcb3" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">charged-particle motion</a> in the surroundings of compact objects.

My research group as well as collaborators continue these endeavors, focusing on <a href="https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.132.085202" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">plasma turbulence in black-hole coronae</a>, accretion <a href="https://doi.org/10.1093/mnras/stae959" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">under the influence of density stratification</a>, etc.
