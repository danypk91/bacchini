Part of my work is dedicated to fundamental plasma processes in the near-Earth environment, studied with first-principles and fluid approaches (or a combination thereof).

Among other works, I have investigated the decay of large-scale Alfvén waves (AWs) into small-scale kinetic AWs (KAWs). This study was <a href="https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.128.025101" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">published in Physical Review Letters</a>. I have also analyzed the <a href="https://doi.org/10.1093/mnras/stae521" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">production of radio waves from basic plasma processes</a>, the <a href="https://doi.org/10.1093/mnras/stae723" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">trapping and acceleration of particles in solar coronal loops</a>, and the <a href="https://doi.org/10.1088/0004-637X/809/1/58" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">propagation of shocks driven by coronal mass ejections</a>.

Several members of my team now continue this line of research under my supervision, studying e.g. <a href="https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0191674" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">plasma mixing at the Earth's magnetosphere</a> and <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.08196" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">kinetic instabilities in the solar wind</a>.
