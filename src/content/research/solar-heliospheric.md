Our group investigates the fundamental processes that govern particle energization in the solar wind and the heliosphere. Using fully kinetic particle-in-cell (PIC) simulations, we study how magnetic reconnection at the heliospheric current sheet drives electron heating and acceleration. We are particularly interested in understanding the energy partition between ions and electrons, the role of turbulence in shaping the solar wind properties, and the mechanisms behind solar energetic particle events. Our simulations are compared with in-situ measurements from spacecraft such as Parker Solar Probe and Solar Orbiter.
