Astrophysical plasmas exhibit dynamics spanning a vast range of spatial and temporal scales, from kinetic micro-scales to global MHD scales. We develop novel numerical techniques that bridge these scales, including implicit PIC schemes, adaptive mesh refinement for kinetic simulations, and hybrid fluid-kinetic methods. A key focus is the development of PIC methods capable of handling sheared, expanding, or escaping plasma configurations — challenges that arise naturally in astrophysical contexts such as accretion disks, stellar winds, and relativistic outflows.
